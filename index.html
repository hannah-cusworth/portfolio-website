About

I'm a history graduate and self-taught programmer currently looking for my first job in the technology sector. Having spent the last few months self-teaching, I'm particularly excited to understand how software is developed in a commercial setting and learn from the expertise of other developers.
I first caught the software bug when I challenged myself to build a website about my Master's thesis. Intrigued, I enrolled on Harvard's Introduction to Computer Science program online, which aims to teach its students to how to think algorithmically and solve problems efficiently over the course of 8 weeks.
Initially, I thought coding might be a challenging new hobby but by the time I finished the course, I had come to realise that programming in fact offered me everything I was looking for in a career: the opportunity to learn something new every day, a collaborative work setting and develop a set of skills with endless problem solving potential. Where my academic studies had always felt introverted and esoteric, the many hours which I have spent coding have by contrast been rewarding and purposeful.
Despite my unconventional academic background, I believe I have a strong aptitude for technical work. In getting to grips with everything from version control to Big O notation, programming has rekindled my love of grappling with logic problems and introduced me to the buzz of designing and creating.
Thank you for taking the time to look at my work, I really appreciate it.

Job Manager

This is a job management web application that I built for my Dad who works as a locksmith. He needed a way of collecting and organising the details of his clients, addresses and job descriptions in one place.

Authentication
Forms
Job Status
Detail Pages
Search
❮ ❯

Authentication: Built using Django's out-of-the-box authentication system. Login is required for all pages except the new job form. No 'sign up'/'forgot password' functionality - all authentication will be controlled by site administrator.

Form: Built using Django's ModelForms. Form validation requires a valid UK postcode and contact number. Billing address form is optional.

Job status: Jobs can be moved between the 'Inbox', 'Current' and 'Archived' pages without reloading the page. Each entry can be clicked to open a details page in a new window.

Detail Pages Detail pages: Client-submitted data is editable. User can navigate between associated jobs, addresses and clients without closing the window.

Search: Search: User can search by person, address or job from within one page. Jobs are searchable by the details the associated address or client fields.

Requirements:

  • Job detail form for recording client contact information, address details and job description in a standardised format.
  • Ability to edit client-submitted data.
  • Track the status of jobs.
  • Explore the relationships between clients, addresses and jobs in an intuitive way.
  • Search functionality, including retrieval of a job based upon the client/address associated with it.
  • Authentication covering whole application except the client form.
